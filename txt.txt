### 🧠 MCX Trading Calculation (Simple Explanation)

In MCX trading (like Silver, Gold, Crude), you don’t buy “1 unit” like stocks.
Instead, you trade in **lots**, and each lot has a fixed **lot size**.

---

### 📦 Key Concepts

* **Price (LTP)** → Current market price
* **Lot Size** → Quantity per lot (e.g., Silver = 30)
* **Lots** → Number of lots user buys

---

### 💰 Total Contract Value

This is the full value of the trade:

> Contract Value = Price × Lot Size × Lots

---

### 💸 Margin (Actual money used)

You don’t pay full value. Only a percentage is blocked:

> Margin ≈ 10%–20% of Contract Value

---

### 📈 Profit & Loss (P&L)

This is the most important calculation:

> P&L = (Sell Price - Buy Price) × Lot Size × Lots

---

### 📊 Example (Silver)

* Buy Price = ₹232600
* Sell Price = ₹233000
* Lot Size = 30
* Lots = 1

> P&L = (233000 - 232600) × 30 = ₹12,000 Profit

---

### ❗ Key Rules

* Always trade in **lots**, not units
* Always multiply by **lot size**
* P&L depends only on **price difference**, not total value
* Margin is used instead of full capital

---

### ✅ Final Summary

> MCX Trading = (Price Difference) × Lot Size × Lots
